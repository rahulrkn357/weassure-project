Network &
Blacklisted Hospitals

What are network hospitals?

Network hospitals are hospitals that have an agreement (tie-up) with your insurance company or TPA. This tie-up means the hospital and insurer have pre-decided (fixed) rates
for certain treatments, so you are not overcharged.
This means:

  • ● You can get cashless treatment
  • ● The hospital and insurer handle payments directly
  • ● You don’t need to pay any amount upfront

Why is it important?

Choosing a network hospital makes your treatment easier, faster and less stressful.
This means:

  • ● Getting quicker approval for treatment
  • ● Reduce paperwork and claim hassles

What are blacklisted hospitals?

Blacklisted hospitals are hospitals that are not approved by your insurance company or TPA. These hospitals are removed from the network either due to no tie-up or some serious issues & policy violations.
Hospitals can be blacklisted for reasons like:

  • ● No tie-up with the insurance company or TPA
  • ● Overcharging patients beyond agreed rates
  • ● History of fraudulent activities or misuse of insurance

When & How to Check?

Best to check the hospital before any kind of treatment or surgery so you don’t face problems later. It can be checked with the following steps:

  • ● Go to the Dashboard
  • ● Click on “Search Hospital” button
  • ● Enter details like your affiliated insurance name, state & city of the hospital or simply enter hospital pincode
  • ✅ If hospital is listed - You’re good to go
  • ❌ If it shows “No hospitals found” - Try another hospital

Remember:

Always check the hospital’s network status before any treatment to avoid claim rejections and payment hassles later.
